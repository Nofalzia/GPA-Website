Next Steps


In Bahria, your overall grade for one subject is divided into four main categories, as illustrated in the graph.


For Low Scoring Students

If your grades haven't been satisfactory, focus your efforts on two key areas:

Assignments and Finals

Even if you struggled throughout the semester, remember that only 50% of your final grade is based on the semester's performance; the remaining 50% depends on the one day that is your final exam.

A useful hack is to ensure you upload EVERY assignment. Instructors often don't have time to review every submission from all students, so uploading all assignments—even if they are done poorly—can still earn you a decent grade. To maximize your scores, put extra effort into the first and last assignments, as these are more likely to be downloaded and closely reviewed by instructors.


For High Scoring Students

If you are already maintaining a good GPA (above 3.00), congratulations! It requires significant effort to achieve this, and your hard work is commendable. Continue to focus on assignments and finals, but also pay attention to quizzes and midterms, as they can further enhance your grade.

However, grades are not everything. I encourage you to seek out life experiences and professional experiences. Visit the international office in Quaid Block for exchange programs, create a LinkedIn profile, attend job fairs if you’re in your 5th or 6th semester, and focus on gaining new experiences.


For Underperforming Students

If you are on probation or at risk of it, choose your subjects wisely by consulting the prerequisite list. Opt for special classes where instructors are generally more lenient. And consider making friends with high achivers and instructors (lifting karain) good luck!


Lastly, make friends with your seniors, they know how the university better then you do.
